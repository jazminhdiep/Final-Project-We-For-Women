WE FOR WOMEN

Employment

Issue:


Despite the shift in traditions and the rate of women pursuing higher education increasing, the gender gap in employment rate is present. Women only make up 49% of the global workforce, and while this may seem like a lot, men make up 75% of the global workforce. This is a difference of 26%, and this is just an average; some countries see a difference of 50% or more. And looking further into the leadership roles women pursue, it is evident that only few women become CEOs of large corporations. As of August 2020, only 13 women were CEOs in Fortune Global 500 companies.

The discrimintiation echoes in the wages as well. In 2018 women earned 12.9% less than men in OECD countries. And this disproportion has not changed, and especially with the negative economic impacts of COVID-19, and disproportion only worsens. In formal jobs women only make 79% of what men make, and in jobs such as domestic work or as a street vendor the ratio decreases to 47%.
